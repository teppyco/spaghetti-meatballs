Feel like eating something Chinese tonight? Why not head to Bao Bei, Vancouver Chinatown’s champion restaurant.

Walking in is a traditional Chinese fare that has merged with French techniques to create amazing and easy to share dishes with your loved ones. You can easily spend your night with a part of four or more for a few hours. Our favorite has got to be the pork belly with sunchokes, Asian cucumber, pickled red onion and star anise tomato sauce. Pair the dishes with a bottle of Spanish wine and you’re set for dinner.

The food is an explosion of flavour and texture for your mouth. The presentation is equally impressive. Dumplings were very unique: the buns were so puffy and delicate yet had a slight dense texture, showcased in an open-faced bun.

The menu is a curious amalgam of Shanghainese, Taiwanese and Vietnamese dishes, many with French inflections (chef Joel Watanabe is half Japanese, half French). But except for a couple of dishes (duck confit congee and wonderfully sharp beef tartare made with lean tenderloin, pickled mustard root and grated ginger), this is not fusion cuisine. It's more of an evolution that improves authentic dishes with high-quality free-range meats, incredibly flavourful organic produce and fresh house-made condiments that aren't packed with preservatives and won't give you any MSG-induced shakes, cramps and headaches.
